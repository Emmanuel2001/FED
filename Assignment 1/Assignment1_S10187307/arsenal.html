List Of Iconic Guns

EMF 1851 Captain Schaeffer

This Limited-edition, .36-caliber 1851 Captain Schaeffer revolver from EMF has a 3.5-Inch barrel, white polymer ultra-ivory grips and lots of laser engraving. This firearm is a reproduction of the one carried by Army Captain F.B. Schaeffer, a veteran of the Maxican-American War and cammanding officer of the Marion Rifles militia.

$395.00

Stoeger Coach Gun

As in the Old West, this shotgun is equipped for security.This shotgun was first developed as a traditional double-trigger model for Cowboy Action Shooting Stoeger's Coach Guns are available in a variety of chamberings with varius deatures, but they have 20-inch barrels. They are available with either single or double triggers. Additinally, customers can choose between the standard A-grade walnut or black-finished Walnut.

$549.00

Traditions Single Actions

Traditions™ offers 1873 Single Action Revolvers made by Pietta in two series with a variety of choices. Both series also come equipped with a transfer bar system which provides the highest level of safety offered in an 1873 single action firearm.

$469.00

Colt Navy Revolver

The Colt Navy revolver is no longer in production. However, Pietta makes a replica, available both with and without an accessory kit. Without the accessory kit, the Model 1851 Confederate Navy .44-Caliber Black Powder Revolver sells for $200. The reproduction can be purchased with the accessory kit for $250.

$250.00

1874 Sharps Rifle

The entire series of Sharps rifles, manufactured from 1848 to 1881, could be considered iconic, but the 1874 edition Sharps ($1,100) stands out from the rest. This 1874 model was a large-bore rifle manufactured to be used with some of the most powerful black powder cartridges in existence at the time.

$1,100.00

Colt Single Action Army Revolver

The Colt Single Action Army Revolver ($1,499), also famously known as the Peacemaker, is probably the single most iconic firearm of the Old West, so much so that it’s called “The Gun That Won the West,” though that phrase originated from Winchester’s marketing of the Model 1873.

$1,499.00

Remington Model 1875

The Remington Model 1875 Single Action Army ($550) was created to compete with Colt’s legendary Peacemaker, but never quite reached the same level of success. Despite this, the Model 1875 was hardly a flop. It quickly gained a reputation as a sturdy reliable firearm, and caught the attention of law enforcement and civilians alike.

$550.00
